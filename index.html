Accomplishments

Isn’t it great when you get that awesome feeling when you know you’ve learned something?

Yesterday I was having trouble understanding Higher-Order Fuctions in Javascript. After reaching out for help, Ben lead me to an excerpt from eloquent Javascript in which they pointed out that the term come from mathematics. After making that connection and going through an example that Scott helped me with…. today it seems so easy! Question myself now as to why I had trouble in the first place.

Well, that is the thing about learning. When you don’t know something, it seems HARD…. but once learned, it is EASY. Comes down to just getting experience in learning what you don’t know.

Today it’s on to some more Javascript. After creating a new function in a program I had already started, I was able to easily use git to make a commit and push it to Github.

Accomplishment!! Looking forward to more of these as the days progress at Fullstack Academy!

Will Learning to Code Empower Me?

Coming from a mathematical background, I’ve spent years building mathematical models to describe the behavior of a system. These systems have ranged from statistics, to biology, and computer science. I’ve used modeling software, including Matlab, to help get a hands on visual model of a system. While doing this, I have realized how much there is to learning about coding.

Matthew Kirschenbaum has written about learning to code in order to understand computers as “engines for creating powerful and persuasive models of the world around us.” I feel that my getting a stronger understanding and capability of coding, it will make me a stronger applied mathematician.

By definition the word empower means to make someone stronger and more confident. By being empowered, we learn more, experience more, and can do more. I look forward to how learning to code is going to empower me.
